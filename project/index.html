Student Academic Success & Dropout Rates

Overall Introduction

Student academic success and dropout rates are critical topics in education research, reflecting the overall effectiveness of educational systems and the well-being of students. Academic success, often measured by grades, graduation rates, or skill acquisition, has a profound impact on a student's future opportunities, career prospects, and socioeconomic mobility. On the other hand, dropout rates serve as an indicator of systemic issues, such as access to resources, student engagement, or the quality of support services. Understanding the factors influencing both academic success and dropout rates is essential not only for improving educational practices but also for shaping policies that foster equitable learning environments. Investigating these topics can offer insights into how schools, teachers, and policymakers can reduce dropout rates, thereby ensuring that more students achieve their full academic potential and contribute meaningfully to society

To learn more about the academic success and dropout rates, you can read this research paper on predicting academic success in higher education or check out a paper from a reputable research organization like Research Gate.

Data Introduction

The dataset used in this analysis focuses on various demographic, academic, and socio-economic factors related to students at the time of their enrollment. The Predict Students Dropout and Academic Success dataset aims to predict student dropout rates and academic success. With a shape of 4,424 rows and 23 columns, this cleaned dataset provides a focused and comprehensive view of the student population under study.

Key features in the dataset include CHANGE TO ONLY THE COLUMNS PEOPLE USED:

  • Marital status, application mode, and course of enrollment: These variables provide insights into students' personal lives and academic choices, offering a better understanding of their trajectories.
  • Previous qualifications and time of attendance (daytime or evening): These factors highlight students' academic history and engagement levels with their program.
  • Parents’ academic background: This includes the highest educational qualifications of the mother and father, as well as their occupations. These socio-economic indicators can significantly influence a student’s academic success.
